Research vision

Physics through language, synthesis through intelligence.

My broader research goal is to make machine intelligence useful for experimental materials science. I am particularly interested in extracting synthesis knowledge from scientific text, using LLMs to address data scarcity, and creating multimodal workflows that connect literature, images, and domain expertise to physically meaningful prediction.

Research interests

A research map organized around materials synthesis, scientific reasoning, and usable AI.

This section is designed as a concept map rather than a keyword list, emphasizing how materials systems, modeling methods, and scientific interfaces connect inside one coherent research program.

Scientific language as experimental signal

Recovering synthesis knowledge from scientific literature so language becomes a structured experimental resource rather than unsearchable narrative.

Prediction under data scarcity

Designing machine learning and LLM workflows for materials problems where data are sparse, heterogeneous, and difficult to curate at scale.

Graphene, diamond, and high-pressure materials

Focusing on materials systems where synthesis pathways, process variables, and domain expertise matter as much as numerical prediction accuracy.

Tools for experimentalists

Building interfaces and scientific AI systems that help researchers reason, retrieve, and generate insight from text, images, and domain knowledge.

Inputs
Literature, process variables, scientific images, synthesis reports
Methods
Machine learning, LLMs, retrieval, multimodal extraction
Materials
High-pressure materials, graphene, diamond, metallurgical systems
Outcome
Experiment-aware predictions and interpretable scientific tools
